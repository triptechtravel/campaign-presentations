NSW Passenger Movement Analysis

Are travellers covering less distance?

Data: CamperMate app GPS data — Jan–Mar 2025 vs Jan–Mar 2026 — New South Wales only

Key Findings

  • ▼ The typical user’s travel distance has collapsed. In March 2025 a typical user covered about 141 km across NSW. In March 2026 that dropped to just 15 km — an 89% fall.
  • ▼ More people are staying put in a single region. 64% of users stayed within one NSW tourism region in March 2026, up from 47% a year earlier.
  • ▼ Even the most active travellers are covering less ground. Among the top 25% of users by distance, the typical trip dropped from 477 km to 314 km — a 34% decline.

Source: CamperMate app GPS location data, geocoded to NSW tourism regions using Destination NSW official boundary files.

Methodology: Travel distance is the straight-line distance (km) between a user’s two furthest-apart GPS positions within NSW for a given month. Only users with 3 or more location readings are included. Tourism region boundaries are official Destination NSW boundaries.

Assumptions & Caveats:

  • CamperMate’s user base skews toward camping and touring travellers (caravanners, road-trippers, backpackers). Results may not be representative of all NSW visitors.
  • We cannot separate intra-NSW locals from interstate or international visitors in these aggregates.
  • Distance is measured as a straight line between the two furthest-apart points, not actual road distance. This means real distances travelled are likely higher than shown.
