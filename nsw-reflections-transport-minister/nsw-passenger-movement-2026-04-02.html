NSW Passenger Movement Analysis

Are travellers covering less distance?

Data: CamperMate app GPS data — Jan–Mar 2025 vs Jan–Mar 2026 — New South Wales only

Key Findings

  • ▼ The typical user’s travel distance has collapsed. Median travel spread fell from 141 km to just 15 km in March — an 89% drop year-on-year.
  • ▼ More people are staying put in a single region. 64% of users stayed within one NSW tourism region in March 2026, up from 47% a year earlier.
  • ▼ Even committed road-trippers are covering less ground. The top 25% of travellers saw their typical distance drop from 477 km to 314 km — a 34% decline.

Source: CamperMate app GPS location data, geocoded to NSW tourism regions using Destination NSW official boundary files.

Methodology: Travel spread measures the straight-line distance (km) between a user’s furthest-apart GPS positions within NSW for a given month. Only users with 3 or more location readings are included. Tourism region boundaries are official Destination NSW polygons.

Assumptions & Caveats:

  • CamperMate’s user base skews toward camping and touring travellers (caravanners, road-trippers, backpackers). Results may not be representative of all NSW visitors.
  • We cannot separate intra-NSW locals from interstate or international visitors in these aggregates.
  • Travel spread is a straight-line measure between the two most distant points, not actual road distance travelled. It provides a lower-bound estimate of total distance covered.
