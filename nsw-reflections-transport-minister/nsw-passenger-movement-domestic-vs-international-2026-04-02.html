NSW Passenger Movement Analysis

Are travellers covering less distance? How do domestic and international visitors compare?

Data: CamperMate app GPS data - Jan–Mar 2025 vs Jan–Mar 2026 - New South Wales only

Key Findings

  • ▼ Domestic users are far less likely to be on a trip. Half of all Australian app users in March 2026 stayed within 8.5 km - suggesting many are browsing the app from home rather than actively travelling, compared to a year earlier when the typical user covered 47 km (indicating at least a day trip or weekend away).
  • ▼ International visitors are also moving less, but still touring. Their typical travel distance fell 44% year-on-year (256 km → 144 km), though most are still visiting multiple regions.
  • ▼ More people are staying put in a single region. 72% of domestic users and 50% of international users stayed within one NSW tourism region in March 2026, up from 60% and 38% respectively.
  • ▼ Even committed road-trippers are covering less ground. Among the most active domestic users (top 25%), typical distances dropped from 280 km to under 150 km - nearly halved.

Source: CamperMate app GPS location data, geocoded to NSW tourism regions using Destination NSW official boundary files.

Methodology: Travel spread measures the straight-line distance (km) between a user’s furthest-apart GPS positions within NSW for a given month. Only users with 3 or more location readings are included. Home country is determined from the user’s device language and region settings. Tourism region boundaries are official Destination NSW polygons.

Assumptions & Caveats:

  • CamperMate’s user base skews toward camping and touring travellers (caravanners, road-trippers, backpackers). Results may not be representative of all NSW visitors.
  • We can identify a user’s home country but not their home state. “Domestic” means an Australian device - we cannot separate intra-NSW locals from interstate visitors.
  • Home country classification for some recent app users is still being processed. These appear as “Unclassified” in the user count chart and are excluded from the domestic/international breakdowns.
  • Travel spread is a straight-line measure between the two most distant points, not actual road distance travelled. It provides a lower-bound estimate of total distance covered.
